# Make Rhythm Visible — Product Foundation

## Core Idea

Build a high-quality music technology app that listens to a rhythm played on snare drum or practice pad, detects the timing of each stroke, and turns that performance into clear visual rhythm notation.

The app should feel simple, beautiful, and almost magical: play something, watch it appear, understand what happened.

## Mission

To help drummers, students, teachers, and musicians turn rhythm into understanding by making percussion performance visual, interactive, and easy to learn from.

## Vision

To make rhythm visible for everyone — from young beginners to experienced musicians — by creating a polished, intuitive tool that captures what someone plays, shows it clearly, and helps them learn, improve, and share their rhythm.

## Product Standard

This app should feel high-quality from the first interaction: simple, elegant, intuitive, and almost magical.

The design should aim for an Apple-level experience: clean visual hierarchy, beautiful motion, minimal friction, thoughtful spacing, polished typography, and an interface that feels obvious without needing explanation.

The product should be easy enough for a child, student, parent, teacher, or musician to use, while still feeling powerful and impressive to someone technical. Every screen should help the user understand rhythm visually, quickly, and beautifully.

The goal is not just to build a functional music tool. The goal is to create something people want to touch, show, and remember.

Every interaction should support one promise:

**Play it. See it. Understand it.**

## Target Users

- Beginner percussion students
- Drumline and snare players
- Music teachers
- Parents helping students practice
- Self-taught musicians
- Creators who want to capture rhythm ideas quickly
- Anyone who understands rhythm better when they can see it

## Product Personality

The app should feel elegant, calm, magical, musical, visual, friendly, high-trust, all-ages, premium, and creative.

It should not feel cluttered, technical, academic, cheap, toy-like, overwhelming, like a generic metronome app, or like a developer demo.

## MVP Goal

Create a polished prototype that proves the magic:

**Record a short rhythm, detect the hits, snap them to a basic rhythmic grid, display the rhythm visually, and show simple snare notation.**

The first version does not need to perfectly handle every possible rhythm. It needs to feel impressive, understandable, and promising.

## First Demo Experience

The first demo should be this simple:

> Open the app. Tap record. Play a rhythm. Watch the rhythm appear.

That is the magic moment.
